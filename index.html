Helpful Faq’s

Sub-metering involves installing additional meters on individual units, departments, or areas within a larger property to measure their specific consumption of utilities such as electricity, water, or gas. This granular measurement allows for detailed tracking and management of utility usage, which can lead to more accurate billing and improved efficiency.

The expense of sub-metering can fluctuate depending on various factors, such as the type of sub-meter, the number of units, and the complexity of the installation. Costs are typically categorized into two primary areas: the cost of the sub-meter equipment and the installation charges. The total cost will be influenced by your specific configuration and the utilities being monitored. When planning your budget for sub-metering, it’s important to account for both the equipment and installation costs.

Accurate billing, cost savings, efficiency, transparency and sustainability

Smart sub-meters have advanced features such as real-time data collection, wireless communication, and integration with building management systems (BMS). They provide instant access to usage data, enable remote monitoring, and often include automated alerts and analytics, while traditional sub-meters generally require manual readings and lack real-time capabilities.
